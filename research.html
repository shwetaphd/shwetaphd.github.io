Energy Storage

Solid-State Electrolyte for All-solid-state Batteries

The objective is to investigate ion transport mechanisms and optimize solid electrolyte chemistries to enable the practical deployment of all-solid-state batteries, utilizing advanced first-principles computational methodologies.

Anode for Post Li-ion Batteries

The objective is to understand the chemical interactions of metal ions beyond lithium and the mechanisms of energy storage through first-principles simulations, aiming to develop low-voltage, high-energy-density anodes.

Energy Conversion

Green Hydrogen & Oxygen Production

The objective is to develop highly efficient catalysts for water splitting via electrocatalysis, photocatalysis, and electro-photocatalysis by employing advanced density functional theory (DFT) and molecular dynamics (MD) simulations to optimize catalyst performance and elucidate reaction mechanisms.

Electrocatalytic Ammonia Production

The aim is to design effective catalysts for ammonia production through the conversion of N2 to NH3 and NO3− to NH3 using computational methods such as ab initio random structure searching (AIRSS) and DFT to discover and refine catalyst properties and reaction pathways, enhancing both efficiency and sustainability.
